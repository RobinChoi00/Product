Nova II is a 3D+ massage chair designed to deliver precise, full-body relief. Its advanced massage mechanism adapts to your body’s contours with integrated 4D control elements, while reaching deep into tired muscles for a more personalized, therapeutic experience. With smooth reclining comfort and intuitive controls, Nova II transforms everyday relaxation into something truly next-level.
3D+ MASSAGE
MECHANISM
3D+ is an enhanced 3D system that integrates selected 4D control elements, delivering smoother motion and more precise pressure control—without the complexity or cost of a full 4D system.
BODY SCAN
TECHNOLOGY
An intelligent body scan adapts seamlessly to your body’s natural contours, guiding the rollers into perfect alignment for a refined, deeply satisfying massage.
3-STAGE
ZERO GRAVITY RECLINING
Experience a feeling of weightlessness with the Osaki Nova II 3-Stage Zero Gravity massage chair. As you recline, your body is evenly supported to reduce back pressure and enhance comfort for a more immersive massage.
SL-TRACK
SYSTEM
Shaped to match the natural curvature of your spine, the SL-track flows from neck to glutes, creating a fluid, deeply satisfying full-back massage.
SPACE-SAVING
TECHNOLOGY
Thoughtfully engineered to save space, the Osaki Nova II slides forward as it reclines, preserving your living area while achieving a full recline with just 8.5 inches of wall clearance.
26-CELL
AIR COMPRESSION
MASSAGE
Twenty-six precisely placed air cells envelop your body in gentle compression, easing tension and deepening relaxation for a more restorative experience.
LUMBAR
HEATING THERAPY
Soothing lumbar heat envelops tired muscles in gentle warmth, releasing tension, improving circulation, and elevating your relaxation experience.
* The heating therapy provides warmth in the range of approximately 75 - 83°F.

MINIMUM DOORWAY & PACKAGING
Measuring Guide
• Calculate the doorway width by subtracting 1 inch from the smallest inner width of the door frame. (1/2 inch from each side)
• Please use a standard measuring tape to measure the doorway.
• If the door cannot be fully opened, open it as much as possible and measure the width from the edge of the door hinge.
• If there is a rounded curve on the wall, calculate the inner width based on the most protruding point of the wall.
